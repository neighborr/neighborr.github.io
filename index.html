Our Mission
Make residential buildings come alive
Let’s face it. Social isolation is a big problem. Especially in cities. So many people, but yet the feeling of being alone is inescapable. As native New Yorkers, we get it. That’s why we created, neighborr.
We are a software platform dedicated to creating a sense of community in residential buildings through nudges designed for neighbors to interact, communicate, and learn with each other. Inspired by the communal atmosphere of hostels, we created neighborr to alleviate social isolation by facilitating community building.
We believe that being connected and adhering to privacy aren't mutually exclusive. That's why new users are verified before being granted access to building communities.

The Team
Built by New Yorkers with global aspirations.
Davis Koh
Co-Founder & Chief Executive Officer
A former Upper Westsider and current Downtown resident, Davis is the visionary and technical talent behind the neighborr product. A graduate of New York University, Davis has software engineering experience at companies like InRythm, Hearst, and SongKick leading technical teams and products before dedicating his time to building neighborr. Davis came up with the idea of creating a sense of community in buildings after spending a few months living in hostels across Southeast Asia and reminiscing about a childhood devoid of social media.
Walter Godinez
Co-Founder & Chief Growth Officer
A native of the Bronx, Walter has known Davis forever and is in charge of growing neighborr’s user base and spreading the neighborr movement. A graduate of New York University and Northwestern University, Walter has a variety of corporate experience at companies like Toyota, Nestle, Goldman Sachs, and Two Sigma leading strategic initiatives before teaming up with Davis. An anthropologist by nature, Walter is particularly interested in the impact neighborr can have on behavioral changes in the way cities interact worldwide.
